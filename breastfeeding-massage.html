Understanding Blocked Milk Ducts

Blocked ducts occur when milk flow is obstructed, often due to stress, improper feeding posture, tight clothing, or fatigue. This common breastfeeding challenge can happen to any nursing mother, but with proper care and attention, it can be effectively managed and prevented.

Common Early Signs of Blocked Ducts:

  • Localized hardness or lump in the breast
  • Pain or warmth to the touch
  • Reduced milk flow or baby's refusal to feed on one side
  • Feeling of heaviness or pressure in the chest
  • Tenderness in specific areas of the breast

When untreated, these symptoms may worsen and cause breast inflammation, redness, and fever, which can be painful and emotionally distressing for new mothers. Early intervention is crucial to prevent the progression to mastitis, a more serious infection that may require medical treatment.
