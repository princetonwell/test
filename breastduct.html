What Is Breast Milk Duct Blockage?

Milk ducts can become blocked when milk is not fully drained, pressure builds up in the breast, or feeding patterns suddenly change. Common signs include:

  • Tender or painful lump in part of the breast
  • Area of warmth, firmness, or swelling
  • Discomfort or sharp pain during feeding or pumping
  • Milk flow that seems slower or reduced on one side

Early care can often relieve the blockage and reduce the chance of developing mastitis. If you develop fever, chills, flu-like symptoms, or intense redness, you should contact your doctor or midwife immediately.

Our service is supportive and complementary and does not replace medical diagnosis or treatment.
